Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The People’s President, The Missile Man of India

Here’s a timeline of Dr. Kalam’s life:

  • 1931 – Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a humble family.
  • 1954 – Graduates in Physics from St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960 – Completes Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960s – Joins the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Works on India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV).
  • 1980 – Leads the successful launch of India’s first satellite, Rohini, into orbit.
  • 1980s–1990s – Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pioneering work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. Heads the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • 1998 – Plays a key role in India’s nuclear tests at Pokhran-II, making India a nuclear power.
  • 2002–2007 – Serves as the 11th President of India, widely called the People’s President for his humility and connection with citizens, especially students.
  • 2011 – Launches the “What Can I Give” mission to inspire youth towards a corruption-free society.
  • 2015 – Passes away at the age of 83 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong living and inspiring until his very last moment.

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."
